Teaching & Courses

Cybersecurity and data analytics education at Air University

Teaching Philosophy

I believe effective education bridges theory and practice. My teaching philosophy emphasizes hands-on experiential learning, where students engage with real-world datasets and modern tools. I integrate research findings into curriculum design, create engaging lab exercises using interactive platforms like Google Colab, and foster critical thinking through problem-based learning.

Beyond technical knowledge, I aim to develop practitioners who understand the broader implications of cybersecurity and data systems in society—including privacy considerations, ethical decision-making, and responsible AI practices.

Network Security

BS Cyber Security, Semester 4

Comprehensive coverage of network security principles, protocols, and practical defense mechanisms in modern networks.

Topics Covered:

  • Network Protocols & OSI Model
  • Cryptographic Fundamentals
  • Firewalls & Intrusion Detection Systems
  • VPN & Secure Communications
  • Network Monitoring & Threat Detection
  • Security Policy & Compliance
  • Penetration Testing Basics
  • Incident Response in Networks

Format: Lecture + Lab + Hands-on exercises

Tools: Wireshark, tcpdump, nmap, Snort, Python networking

Assessment: Projects, labs, assignments, final examination

Data Analytics

MS Cyber Psychology, Semester 2

Introduction to data analysis techniques, visualization, statistical methods, and exploratory data analysis with hands-on Python programming.

Topics Covered:

  • Data Fundamentals & Preprocessing
  • Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
  • Statistical Analysis & Hypothesis Testing
  • Data Visualization Techniques
  • Python for Data Analysis (Pandas, NumPy)
  • Time-Series Analysis
  • Clustering & Pattern Discovery
  • Case Studies & Real-World Datasets

Format: Lecture + Google Colab Labs + Group Projects

Tools: Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, Scikit-learn, Google Colab

Assessment: Lab notebooks, data analysis projects, final presentation

Professional Practices

BS Cyber Security, Semester 8

Capstone course preparing students for professional careers in cybersecurity through practical project work and industry-relevant practices.

Topics Covered:

  • Professional Ethics in Cybersecurity
  • Security Architecture & Design
  • Governance, Risk & Compliance (GRC)
  • Project Management in Security
  • Communication & Technical Writing
  • Career Development & Specializations
  • Current Trends & Emerging Threats
  • Capstone Project Execution

Format: Seminar + Capstone Project + Industry Guest Speakers

Tools: Industry tools, case studies, real-world scenarios

Assessment: Capstone project, presentations, professional portfolio

Learning Outcomes & Student Development

🎯 Technical Mastery

Students gain hands-on proficiency with industry-standard tools and technologies in cybersecurity and data analytics.

🔍 Critical Thinking

Development of analytical skills to identify, assess, and solve complex security and data challenges.

📊 Data Literacy

Ability to work with data effectively—from collection and analysis to visualization and interpretation.

💼 Professional Readiness

Preparation for careers through capstone projects, case studies, and engagement with current industry practices.
